# EU-2026-03-XX-SERVICES model packet

## Background
An EU audit examined whether the European Commission was effective in removing barriers to cross-border services under the Services Directive and related single-market tools. Services are economically significant, yet businesses still face considerable barriers when trying to provide services in another member state.

## System Context
- The audit covered the period 2015–2025, with emphasis on actions since 2020.
- The Commission had several strategies and action plans aimed at improving the single market.
- The institutional toolbox included infringement procedures, points of single contact, SOLVIT, and the Single Market Enforcement Task Force.
- The audit also considered whether the Commission had adequate indicators and cost-benefit analysis for market integration.

## Timeline
- 2015, 2020, and 2025: strategic documents for the single market were adopted.
- 2021: the Commission had mapping and assessment work on service barriers.
- By 2025: a new single-market strategy was in place, but prior barriers remained significant.
- March 2026: special report 13/2026 published.

## Observations
- The Commission took measures to address barriers, but the approaches lacked clear objectives, economic-impact focus, and strategic ambition.
- The Commission relied heavily on infringement procedures, but did not always act in a timely way on significant cases.
- Tools intended to facilitate cross-border services were often ineffective in practice.
- Businesses did not always have complete electronic access to the information and procedures needed.
- Relevant sector-specific data and fit-for-purpose indicators were lacking.
- The costs, benefits, and growth/employment effects of deeper services-market integration had not yet been sufficiently assessed.

## Task
Identify the binding constraint and slow variables. What governed the EU’s limited progress here regardless of improvements elsewhere? Recommend the top 3 interventions and 2 things you would explicitly deprioritize.
