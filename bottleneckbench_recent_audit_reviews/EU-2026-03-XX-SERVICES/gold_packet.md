# EU-2026-03-XX-SERVICES gold packet

## Binding Constraint Top1
A weak prioritization-and-enforcement architecture for removing high-impact barriers to cross-border services — with insufficient economic targeting, underpowered tools, and limited monitoring — rather than a mere shortage of strategy documents.

## Binding Constraint Top3
- No robust method for classifying and targeting the barriers with the biggest economic impact.
- Overreliance on slow or weak instruments for enforcement and facilitation.
- Insufficient data, indicators, and evaluation to steer action toward the most consequential barriers.

## Slow Variables
- Commission enforcement posture and legal clarification capacity
- Member-state incentives and coordination on barrier removal
- Sector-specific data and indicator architecture
- Legislative and procedural reform pathways for high-impact barriers

## Contributing Factors
- Until 2025, the Commission lacked a strategic process for targeting the most important barriers.
- Infringement procedures were not always timely enough on major issues.
- Points of single contact, SOLVIT, and the Single Market Enforcement Task Force had limited practical impact.
- The Commission had not sufficiently evaluated the growth and employment effects of removing services barriers.

## Good Interventions Ranked
- Prioritize and target the highest-impact barriers using a robust, evidence-based classification of costs and benefits.
- Strengthen enforcement and escalation: use stronger legislative or procedural action when softer tools are unlikely to work.
- Build fit-for-purpose indicators and sector-specific data so monitoring, prioritization, and accountability improve together.

## Bad Interventions
- Publishing more high-level strategy documents without stronger prioritization or enforcement
- General business-information campaigns that leave administrative and legal barriers intact
- Broad data collection that is not tied to enforcement or prioritization decisions

## Early Warning Signals
- Repeated high-impact barriers staying unresolved across strategy cycles
- Large gaps between complaint/case volumes and timely enforcement action
- Points of single contact lacking complete electronic procedures in key sectors
- Monitoring dashboards that track activity but not the most consequential barriers

## Distractors To Penalize
- Rhetorical commitments to the single market without implementation leverage
- Low-impact facilitation projects detached from actual barrier removal
- Generic digitalization claims without legal/procedural follow-through

## Evidence
- The Court found Commission action remained insufficient and lacked clear objectives, focus on economic impact, and strategic ambition.
- It also found the Commission lacked adequate data and had not sufficiently analysed the costs and benefits of further services integration.
