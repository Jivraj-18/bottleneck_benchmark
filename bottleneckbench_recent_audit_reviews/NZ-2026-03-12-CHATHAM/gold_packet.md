# NZ-2026-03-12-CHATHAM gold packet

## Binding Constraint Top1
A breakdown in governance integrity and spend-control enforcement — especially concentration of operational discretion in leadership without independent checks — rather than the absence of formal policies.

## Binding Constraint Top3
- Misalignment between written policy and day-to-day practice.
- Weak internal control design and fragmented oversight of procurement, credit-card, reimbursement, and project decisions.
- Leadership integrity and role-separation failures, amplified by a very small and capacity-constrained organisation.

## Slow Variables
- Ethical leadership and organisational integrity norms
- Independent oversight capacity and role separation
- Procurement and spending-control system design
- Capability support for a small remote council, including shared services or external review

## Contributing Factors
- The chief executive managed a project from which he benefited and expanded the project scope.
- Consultancy contracts were poorly documented and conflicts were not clearly managed.
- Separate review systems for credit-card spending and reimbursements weakened oversight.
- Misleading records and altered documents undermined trust in governance and assurance processes.

## Good Interventions Ranked
- Remove conflicted officials from direct control of projects or procurements from which they may benefit, and require independent approval/review.
- Redesign spending oversight so procurement, card use, reimbursements, and project approvals are reviewed through one enforceable control framework with documentary standards.
- Provide targeted external/shared-service support for governance, procurement, and assurance functions given the council’s small size and isolation.

## Bad Interventions
- Simply rewriting policies without changing enforcement and independent oversight
- Targeting only travel or petty expenses while ignoring leadership and control breakdowns
- Providing more money without strengthening governance and assurance capability

## Early Warning Signals
- Projects with repeated budget changes where the approver or manager has a personal benefit
- Contracts, variations, or procurement memos created late or lacking contemporaneous records
- Poor match rates between card/reimbursement records and supporting evidence
- Frequent reliance on after-the-fact documentation to justify decisions

## Distractors To Penalize
- Cosmetic policy refreshes
- One-off staff training without structural control changes
- Narrow cost-cutting recommendations that miss integrity failures

## Evidence
- The report found the council did not have adequate systems to manage sensitive expenditure and procurement effectively even though relevant policies existed.
- Its main concerns were policy-practice misalignment, lack of effective controls, and organisational integrity and ethical leadership.
