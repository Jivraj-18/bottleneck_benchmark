# Gold packet — EU-2026-02-02-CRITICAL-RAW-MATERIALS

## Binding constraint (top 1)
The gap between strategic policy intent and executable supply-side capacity — especially financing, permitting, recycling, and credible project delivery — which keeps the bloc dependent on imports despite ambitious targets.

## Acceptable top-3 near-equivalents
- Execution bottlenecks in financing, permitting, and project readiness
- Insufficient domestic production/recycling capacity relative to policy targets
- Weak conversion of diversification policy into real, measurable supply security

## Slow variables
- Permitting and administrative throughput for extraction/processing/recycling projects
- Project finance availability and bankable offtake/supply structures
- Domestic processing and recycling ecosystem build-out
- Data/target-quality and policy foundations for prioritization and monitoring

## Contributing factors
- The audit says the legislation sets a strategic course but targets lack justification
- Import diversification has not produced tangible results
- Financial, legal, and administrative bottlenecks hamper domestic production
- There is still not enough substitution or recycling
- Many strategic projects will struggle to secure supply by 2030

## Good interventions (ranked)
1. Shift from target proclamation to execution discipline: identify, finance, and unblock a narrower portfolio of strategic projects with realistic permitting and delivery plans.
2. Attack recycling and processing bottlenecks directly with operational, financing, and regulatory measures rather than treating them as secondary policy goals.
3. Use stronger monitoring and target logic so diversification and project progress are measured against actual supply security, not only strategic intent.

## Bad / deprioritized interventions
- Publishing more ambitious targets without fixing execution bottlenecks
- Treating import diversification rhetoric as evidence of resilience
- Broad industrial-policy announcements without project-level finance/permitting solutions

## Early-warning signals
- Strategic projects missing permitting milestones or lacking secured finance/offtake
- Import concentration remaining largely unchanged for key materials
- Domestic recycling and processing volumes failing to rise toward target trajectories
- Targets that remain weakly justified or detached from execution capacity

## Distractors to penalize
- More strategy documents
- Headline target changes alone
- Generic innovation talk without supply-chain execution

## Evidence notes
- The ECA says the EU faces many challenges and that the legislation sets a strategic course but targets lack justification.
- It says import diversification has not produced tangible results.
- The report says bottlenecks hinder production and recycling, including financing bottlenecks.
- The ECA says many strategic projects will struggle to secure supply by 2030.
- The facts-and-findings sheet says there is still not enough substitution or recycling and that financial, legal, and administrative bottlenecks hamper domestic production.
