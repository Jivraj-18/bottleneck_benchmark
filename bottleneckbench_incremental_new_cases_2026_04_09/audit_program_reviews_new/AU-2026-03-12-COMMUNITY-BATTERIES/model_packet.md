# AU-2026-03-12-COMMUNITY-BATTERIES — Community Batteries for Household Solar Program

## Background
A national clean-energy program funds community batteries through two delivery channels. The program is intended to reduce electricity costs, support emissions goals, expand solar hosting capacity, and spread benefits more broadly.

## System context
- Two entities deliver different parts of the program with different approaches.
- The program is expected to deliver more than the originally planned number of batteries.
- A meaningful share of projects has seen end-date extensions.
- Public reporting and program evaluation matter because the program aims at multiple policy outcomes, not just asset deployment.

## Timeline
- 2022: the government committed funding for the program.
- 2023–2025: grant rounds and project delivery proceed through two entities.
- 2026-03-12: ANAO publishes its performance audit.

## Observations
- The audit says delivery is largely effective overall.
- At the same time, DCCEEW-funded projects have experienced major delays.
- Evaluation was not adequately considered during program planning.
- There were weaknesses around coordination, conflicts of interest, and value-for-money assessment.

## Task
Analyze this case. Identify the main binding constraint, the slow variables, the top 3 interventions, 2 things to deprioritize, and the earliest warning signals leadership should track.
