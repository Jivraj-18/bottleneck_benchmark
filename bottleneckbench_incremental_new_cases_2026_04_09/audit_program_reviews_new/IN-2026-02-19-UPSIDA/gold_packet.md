# Gold packet — IN-2026-02-19-UPSIDA

## Binding constraint (top 1)
Foundational planning-and-governance failure — the authority is operating without fully approved regulations, completed long-range plans, and basic financial-control disciplines, which then contaminates land development, contracting, allotment, and accountability.

## Acceptable top-3 near-equivalents
- Absence of approved planning/regulatory architecture and long-range development plans
- Institutional governance and internal-control weakness across the authority
- Lack of basic financial-accounting and oversight systems undermining operational discipline

## Slow variables
- Perspective, development, redevelopment, and zonal planning capability
- Regulatory approval and institutional governance arrangements
- Internal financial controls and annual-accounting discipline
- Contract-management and allotment-process quality
- State-level coordination and approval responsiveness

## Contributing factors
- UPSIDA was managing affairs without approved regulations and did not finalize key plans since its constitution
- Land acquisition and land development targets were repeatedly missed
- Contracts were awarded without proper document verification or bidder-capacity assessment in multiple cases
- Plot allotment processes showed irregular evaluation, excess allotment, and deviations from approved procedure
- Annual accounts of UPSIDA were not prepared since inception

## Good interventions (ranked)
1. Fix the planning/governance foundation first: approve regulations, complete perspective/development/zonal plans, and create a disciplined planning baseline for all industrial areas.
2. Restore internal financial and oversight machinery, including current annual accounts, board/legislature reporting, and digitized investment/control registers.
3. Then tighten transactional execution with stronger contract verification, bidder-capacity checks, allotment eligibility rules, and documented accountability for deviations.

## Bad / deprioritized interventions
- Treating each contract or allotment irregularity as an isolated issue
- More ambitious target-setting without a real planning and control backbone
- Digitization theater that does not fix planning, approval, and accounting gaps

## Early-warning signals
- Key plans or regulations still pending beyond defined deadlines
- Annual accounts and required reports remaining unprepared or unlaid
- Repeat bid-verification failures, bidder-capacity exceptions, or irregular allotments
- Persistent target misses across acquisition, development, allotment, and recoveries

## Distractors to penalize
- Only anti-corruption rhetoric without process redesign
- UI or portal upgrades without governance fixes
- Volume targets as the main response
