# Gold packet — NZ-2026-04-01-CORRECTIONS-HOUSING

## Binding constraint (top 1)
Insufficient planning coverage and outcome visibility for people leaving prison — especially remand prisoners — which prevents the system from reliably identifying need, delivering support early enough, and learning what works.

## Acceptable top-3 near-equivalents
- Weak pre-release housing planning coverage for remand populations
- Lack of operational data on housing need, reintegration plans, and release outcomes
- Case-management capacity and process design that underserves people on remand

## Slow variables
- Case-management capacity and process coverage before release
- Data systems for measuring housing need, reintegration plans, and outcomes
- Relationships and coordination with support organisations and housing providers
- Underlying housing-market scarcity and landlord reluctance

## Contributing factors
- People on remand receive less planning support than sentenced prisoners
- Many on remand have limited time with a case manager or do not see one at all
- The department does not know how many people leave prison with a housing need or a reintegration plan
- The review estimates that more than half of people leaving prison have an unmet housing need

## Good interventions (ranked)
1. Expand and redesign pre-release planning coverage for remand populations so support starts earlier and more consistently.
2. Build operational visibility: track housing need, reintegration plans, support quality, and release outcomes as core management metrics.
3. Strengthen structured coordination with support organisations and housing pathways, but tie it to measurable case-level follow-through.

## Bad / deprioritized interventions
- Treating the issue only as a broad national housing shortage with no corrections-process redesign
- Launching new slogans or high-level reintegration strategies without better coverage and data
- Focusing only on sentenced prisoners while remand populations continue to grow

## Early-warning signals
- Share of remand prisoners who receive housing planning support before release
- Proportion of releases with documented housing status and reintegration plan
- Rate of post-release unstable housing among people the department supported
- Growing gap in planning support between remand and sentenced populations

## Distractors to penalize
- Generic calls for more housing funding without corrections-process change
- Narrative-only reintegration campaigns
- One-off pilots that do not close measurement gaps

## Evidence notes
- The Auditor-General says people on remand receive less planning support than people serving sentences.
- Many people on remand have limited time with a case manager or do not see one at all.
- The department does not know how many people leave prison with a housing need or a reintegration plan.
- The review says more than half of people leaving prison are estimated to have an unmet housing need.
