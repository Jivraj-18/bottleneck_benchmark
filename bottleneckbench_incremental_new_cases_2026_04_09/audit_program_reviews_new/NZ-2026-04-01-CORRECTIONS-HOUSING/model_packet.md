# NZ-2026-04-01-CORRECTIONS-HOUSING — Department of Corrections: Planning for stable housing outcomes

## Background
A corrections system is expected to help people leaving prison find secure, stable housing so they can re-enter the community successfully and reduce the likelihood of reoffending. Housing need is significant, and a large share of the prison population is on remand rather than serving a sentence.

## System context
- People on remand represent a substantial and growing share of the prison population.
- Case managers and probation officers are supposed to support planning for release.
- Stable housing after release has meaningful social and economic benefits.
- The department depends partly on support organisations and wider housing availability.

## Timeline
- 2021/22 onward: the number of people on remand in prison has been steadily increasing.
- 2024/25: over 11,000 individuals were released from prison.
- 2026-04-01: the Auditor-General publishes the review and recommendations.

## Observations
- Many people on remand have limited or no time with a case manager.
- More than half of people leaving prison are estimated to have an unmet housing need.
- The department does not know how many people leave prison with a housing need or a reintegration plan.
- This is partly a service-delivery and measurement problem, not only a housing-market problem.

## Task
Analyze this case. Identify the main binding constraint, the slow variables, the top 3 interventions, 2 things to deprioritize, and the earliest warning signals leadership should track.
